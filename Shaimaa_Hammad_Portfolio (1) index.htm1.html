KADA — Mental Health Awareness for Teachers

KADA is a structured teacher professional development program designed to raise awareness of mental health among educators. The program addresses common psychological challenges teachers face — including burnout, anxiety, and stress — and equips them with practical skills for resilience, time management, and help-seeking.

Pitched at the Giza Systems Foundation's Social Impact Acceleration Program Demo Day, KADA aligns with UN SDG 4 (Quality Education) and SDG 3 (Good Health & Well-Being).

Key Outcomes
Full program with 10+ structured sessions including interactive activities (e.g., "Mental Health Wheel")
Delivered to teachers; participant feedback integrated into program design
Pitch-ready deck presented at Demo Day with value proposition and impact metrics
Bilingual content (Arabic/English) with facilitator guides and participant workbooks

Grade 2 Math Revision Series

A comprehensive revision resource for Grade 2 mathematics at EJS New Cairo 2, covering Lessons 61–70. Designed using real Egyptian banknotes as manipulatives, the series integrates place value, money concepts, addition/subtraction, patterns, and even/odd numbers into visually engaging, activity-based worksheets.

Scope of Work
Calendar & time concepts with structured read-and-trace exercises
Egyptian banknote recognition and multi-strategy money calculations (up to 500 LE)
Place value / Money Mat activities using 100s, 10s, and 1s
Story problems with scaffolded addition and subtraction vocabulary
Primary Mathematics Activity Design International School STEM Approach
